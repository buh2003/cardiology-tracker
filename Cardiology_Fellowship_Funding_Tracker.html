📋 General Strategy

Start early, aim big. The ACC/ABC Merck and HRS scholarships are the most fellow-friendly large awards — apply in Years 1–2 to fund a protected Year 3 research block.


Society membership matters. Join ACC, AHA, and the specialty society most aligned with your research (HRS, HFSA, SCAI, ASNC, ASE) at the start of fellowship.


The K-award timeline. NIH K08/K23 take 12–18 months to write well. Start developing specific aims by mid-Year 2 so you can submit before or during your research year.


Parallel tracking. Many fellows secure a society fellowship award to fund their research year while simultaneously developing their NIH K-award. These are complementary, not competing.


Preliminary data wins. Even small pilot studies or retrospective analyses significantly strengthen NIH applications.

🏆 Priority by Research Track

🫀 Clinical / Outcomes:

  • ACC/ABC Merck ($100K)
  • NIH K23 ($150–180K/yr, 3–5 yrs)
  • AHA Postdoc → AHA Career Development Award
  • HFSA Fellowship Grant (if HF focus)

🔬 Basic Science / Translational:

  • ACC/ABC Merck ($100K)
  • HRS Research Fellowship – Basic track ($75K)
  • NIH K08 (mentored clinical scientist)
  • NIH K99/R00 (pathway to independence)
  • Burroughs Wellcome CAMS ($700K)

⚡ Electrophysiology:

  • HRS Research Fellowship (best fit)
  • ACC/ABC Merck + NIH K award in parallel

🖼️ Imaging (Echo / Nuclear):

  • ASE EDGES Grant or Douglas Scholar Award
  • ASNC Clinical Grant / Discover Grant
  • ASNC/Pfizer Amyloidosis Award (if amyloid focus)
